Altes Museum

Significance: Greco-Roman Lineage

A masterpiece of Neoclassical architecture. Represents the Greco-Roman heritage used to establish cultural and political legitimacy for the Prussian state.

Neues Museum

Significance: Tribal Roots & National Mythology

Home to the Egyptian collection (including Nefertiti) and prehistoric artifacts. Focuses on tribal roots and national mythology, tracing the early origins of the Germanic peoples.

Alte Nationalgalerie

Significance: Early 19th-Century Nationality

Captures early 19th-century nationality through the lens of Romanticism and the rise of Imperial power. Home to works by Caspar David Friedrich and the German Romantics.

Bode-Museum

Significance: Late 19th-Century Imperial Cosmopolitanism

Highlights late 19th-century imperial cosmopolitanism and the enduring influence of the Christian era on European art. Byzantine art and Renaissance sculptures.
